Design Sprint 1 Day 1 - Research and Brainstorm Ideas

The purpose of this website will be twofold - as a portfolio, and as a way for me to explore whatever I'm curious about. Given that this is going to be part portfolio and part blog, I should consider the audience that I want to attract. Below is a list of potential audiences I assume would be attracted to my website over the short, medium, and long term.

Short-term (1-3 years)
  • Potential employees / clients / recruiters
  • Friends and family
  • Other people who are interested in my website
Mid-term (3-10 years)
  • Industry peers
  • Peers that are interested in my work
  • Visitors who are interested in my projects
Long-term (10+ years)
  • Established audience who are interested in science made accessible
  • Industry professionals or recruiters offering senior positions

As of now, I will only focus on what I believe to be the two biggest audiences - potential employees and general audiences who are interested in my work.


Down below is a rough psychologocial profile of the two main audiences that I want to attract.
Criteria General Recruiter General Audience
Digital Literacy High proficiency with online tools and platforms Varied levels, depending on age, education, and tech exposure
Goal Orientation Highly focused on finding suitable candidates efficiently Diverse objectives (information-seeking, entertainment, etc.)
Time Sensitivity Works under pressure to fill positions quickly Browses at a more leisurely pace without strict time constraints
Attention to Detail Pays close attention to specific qualifications and nuances Tends to skim content, focusing on highlights or key points
Decision-Making Process Systematic, criteria-based approach Quicker, more intuitive decisions based on personal preferences
Content Preferences Detailed, professional content (resumes, work histories) Diverse, easily digestible formats (videos, infographics, articles)
Trust Factors Values professional credentials, detailed work experiences Relies on user reviews, brand reputation, overall website design

Since this site would get little traffic at the beginning, I am going to focus on recruiters, and prioritize ease of navigation for the website. According to this website, most recruiters look for character, creativity, dedication, and passion in a website. I don’t think I would need to show my credentials, that would be the purpose of my resume. Since the point of this website is to showcase my passions, I think that would also be fine. Therefore, I'll focus on simplicity and ease of navigation for everyone. This is also very important, as according to this survey, a recruiter only looks for 55 seconds on average at a resume and portfolio website combined.

Main Ideas for this Design Sprint

  • Simplicity - This site will be for publishing ideas, not maintaining a complex website. Keep It Simple, Stupid!
  • Ease of Navigation - If the audience can't navigate it, they won't stay.
  • Revamping general style - Don't deliberately aim for a minimalist style from the beginning, fill it up with content I like, then remove ones I don't need
