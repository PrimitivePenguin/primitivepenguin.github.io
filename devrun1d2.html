Design Sprint 1 - Wireframing and some style changes

Wireframing

Created a wireframe of the home page, a general article format, and a forecasting format. The home page is a generic design from a YouTube video on the 'best' hero section. I don't have any experience with designing websites, so this is a good start. The general article section is a simple format with a title, date, article itself, and a table of contents on the left side. The forecasting page aims to emulate a Google search, where instead of a link, there is a forecast.

Testing Different Typography

Currently, I do not have many pictures. As such, good typography would have an outstanding effect on the website. After some research, I've chosen a couple of fonts. Two options use sans-serif fonts for the heading, providing clear legibility, while the body uses serif fonts for a more authoritative tone, which my website lacks. I will be testing different fonts and font sizes to see which one looks the best. I find it interesting that one group of friends has told me that sans-serif fonts are easier to look at and that serif fonts such as Lora look 'old'. However, another group of friends who are mostly in Business, have told me that considering my aim to appeal to recruiters, the serif fonts for the body and sans-serif for the header is better. I will ask them again for feedback after most of the new website design is implemented.
